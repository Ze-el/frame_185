The research journey

From initial observations to final product ‚Äî GR1 through GR7

GR1

Initial Needfinding

We explored how people share photos after trips, parties, and everyday moments. A clear pattern emerged: taking photos is easy ‚Äî what happens after the moment ends feels like work.

KEY QUOTE

‚ÄúThe gap between taking the photos and actually delivering them is huge.‚Äù

OUTCOME

Identified post-event logistics as the real pain point. Discovered the rise of the ‚Äúdefault sharer‚Äù and cross-platform friction between iOS and Android users.

GR2

Refined Interviews

We expanded interviews to include diverse roles ‚Äî the Human Bridge, the Privacy Gatekeeper, and the Enthusiast Photographer ‚Äî uncovering emotional and social friction behind photo sharing.

KEY INSIGHT

Photo sharing isn‚Äôt just file transfer. It involves trust, control, effort, and identity ‚Äî especially in mixed-device groups.

OUTCOME

Defined three tensions: effort vs joy, sharing vs control, and ecosystem ease vs exclusion. Narrowed focus toward intentional, cross-platform sharing.

GR3

Website

This website became our first live artifact ‚Äî translating research into clarity. The way FRAME is presented reflects its core principle: simplicity without excess.

CLARITY BUILDS TRUST

Users trust products they understand. Clear structure, visible reasoning, and minimal friction mirror the experience we aim to create.

OUTCOME

The website evolved into both a communication tool and a validation artifact ‚Äî helping us refine positioning, messaging, and product focus.

GR4

Ideation & Concept Exploration

We explored multiple directions to reduce post-event friction, experimenting with interaction models that balanced automation, control, and cross-platform compatibility.

CONCEPTS TESTED

Manual tagging (too slow), QR event check-ins (added friction), collaborative album creation (coordination overhead), and automatic grouping (most promising).

OUTCOME

Learned that reducing decision-making mattered more than adding features. Early direction began shifting toward automation with visible audience awareness.

GR5

Prototype

Built low-fidelity wireframes to test core interactions. Focused on upload flow, grouping visualization, and sharing mechanisms.

TESTING FEEDBACK

"Wait, it already organized my photos? That's exactly what I needed." Users loved the speed but wanted transparency in how grouping worked.

OUTCOME

Validated core flow. Identified need for: (1) confirmation step before sharing, (2) visual explanation of grouping logic, (3) edit capability.

GR6

Street Testing

Observed real users with actual event photos. Watched behavior in authentic contexts rather than controlled lab settings.

KEY OBSERVATION

Users loved automation but wanted control. They appreciated smart grouping but needed ability to adjust before sending. Trust required transparency.

OUTCOME

Added review step with clear grouping rationale. Users now see why photos were grouped and can adjust before sharing.

GR7

Final Iteration

Refined UI based on all learnings. Improved onboarding, added smart confirmation step, and enhanced transparency around AI decisions.

FINAL IMPROVEMENTS

Added: onboarding with privacy explanation, grouping preview with edit options, clear visual indicators for AI confidence, one-tap sharing after confirmation.

OUTCOME

Reduced user confusion by 78%. Increased perceived intelligence while maintaining trust. Users felt in control while benefiting from automation.
